# Signal Taxonomy

A structured vocabulary for classifying signals in the Sintetica trend observatory.

## Primary categories

### Regulatory & Policy
Signals related to laws, regulations, government policy, enforcement patterns.
- Examples: AI Act implementation, data localization requirements, procurement rules
- Watch: legislative calendars, regulatory agency announcements, enforcement cases

### Technological
New tools, platforms, or capabilities crossing mainstream adoption thresholds.
- Examples: LLM capabilities reaching new benchmarks, new deployment patterns, open-source releases
- Watch: adoption curves, enterprise uptake, cost curves

### Behavioral
Shifts in how clients, markets, or users are actually acting (revealed preference over stated).
- Examples: clients asking for AI in RFPs, consultant roles evolving, meeting formats changing
- Watch: RFP language, hiring patterns, budget allocations

### Economic & Business Model
Pricing dynamics, funding patterns, cost structure shifts, revenue model changes.
- Examples: consulting rate compression, AI-augmented service pricing, new bundling
- Watch: competitor pricing, VC funding themes, M&A activity

### Social & Cultural
Values shifts, demographic changes, generational differences in expectations.
- Examples: demand for transparency in AI use, sustainability expectations, remote-first norms
- Watch: survey data, media coverage volume, language in public discourse

### Competitive & Ecosystem
New entrants, exits, pivots, partnership formations in Sintetica's adjacent markets.
- Examples: big 4 AI practices scaling, boutique AI strategy firms emerging
- Watch: LinkedIn hiring, firm announcements, conference speakers

## Signal strength scale

| Strength | Criteria | Action |
|----------|----------|--------|
| **Weak** | Single data point, anecdotal, could be noise | Capture and tag; revisit in 90 days |
| **Emerging** | 2-3 independent data points, pattern forming | Monitor actively; look for confirming/disconfirming |
| **Confirmed** | 3+ independent sources, reproducible, directional | Promote to trend; assess strategic implications |

## Horizon definitions

| Horizon | Timeframe | Planning use |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| 0-1yr | Now to 12 months | Immediate positioning, current proposals |
| 1-3yr | 1 to 3 years | Capability building, service design |
| 3-5yr | 3 to 5 years | Scenario planning, strategic bets |
| 5yr+ | Beyond 5 years | Long-range foresight, scenario axes |

## Domain-specific watches for Sintetica

**AI & Design consulting:**
- AI capabilities replacing vs. augmenting design thinking
- Client sophistication curves (what they now expect vs. need explanation on)
- Emergence of AI-native competitors with lower overhead

**Strategy & Foresight:**
- Demand for structured foresight vs. ad hoc futurism
- Scenario planning methodology evolution
- New data sources for strategic intelligence (alt data, satellite, etc.)

**Latin America & Colombia specifically:**
- Digital economy regulation timeline
- Government AI adoption pace
- Client readiness for AI-augmented advisory services
